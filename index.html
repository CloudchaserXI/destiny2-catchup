Long ago, a vast white sphere, the Traveler, drifted into our solar system and gifted humanity a Golden Age of impossible science, before its ancient enemy, the Darkness, caught it. In the cataclysm called the Collapse, the dying Traveler released the Light and the Ghosts, sparks that raise the dead as Guardians, immortal soldiers who defend the Last City beneath the Traveler's silent husk.

You are one of them. Your commanders are the Vanguard: the Titan , the Warlock , and the Hunter . What follows is the story of the last decade, told for someone who's dipped in and out, with the focus on the moments that actually changed the universe: who died, who switched sides, and which worlds were lost or won.

The whole saga in 90 seconds

The Cabal's caged the Traveler and lost; that woke it and summoned its ancient enemy, . Over ten years you lost , learned the truth that Light and Dark aren't good and evil, mastered the Darkness yourself, and (alongside humanity, the Eliksni and the Cabal) finally killed the Witness inside the Traveler. But its death cracked the cosmic order. Now the are dying and divided, a buried prophecy says they must be bound to survive, and the Vanguard has fractured over hiding the truth. Monument of Triumph is the game's send-off and its two hidden cutscenes are the real last word. The detail's all below; hover any name for who they are.

Before Destiny 2 · the first years

If you only ever played Destiny 2, the first game laid down three years of groundwork. After the Collapse, the survivors built the Last City under the silent Traveler, and the sent Guardians out to hold back the species picking over the ruins: the scavenging Fallen, the militaristic Cabal, the machine , and the . One early find mattered more than the rest. Buried beneath Mars sat , a Golden-Age war machine that had ridden out the Collapse on its own terms and spent years deciding whether humanity was worth protecting.

Out past the City, in the asteroid belt, lived the , a people apart who answered to Queen and her brother, Prince . Their hidden kingdom, the Reef, is where Guardians first met them. When the Fallen House of Wolves rose up under a captain called , who wanted to unite every Fallen House under one banner, Mara opened the Reef to Guardians and they put him down.

The larger threat was the Hive. Guardians killed the Hive prince Crota, then faced his father, , the Taken King, named for his power to twist enemies into a warped, enslaved force called the Taken. He brought his moon-sized Dreadnaught into the system to avenge his son. Mara led the Awoken fleet against Oryx and was presumed dead when the Dreadnaught's weapon wiped out her armada. No body was ever found. That apparent death, and Uldren's slow unravelling without her, is what sets up Forsaken. Keep Rasputin, the Awoken, Skolas and Oryx in the back of your mind, because all four come back around in Destiny 2.

Saga One · 2017 to 2024

The Light & Darkness Saga

The ten-year war that began with a stolen Light and ended inside the Traveler itself.

The Red War · 2017 Light restored

Ghaul Cages the Light

, commander of the Cabal Red Legion, does what no enemy dared: he cages the Traveler and severs every Guardian from the Light. Stripped and nearly killed, you flee to the European Dead Zone, commune with a wild Shard of the Traveler, and claw your Light back.

You retake the City. When Ghaul tries to seize the Light by force, the Traveler wakes and obliterates him in a burst that ripples out across the galaxy as a signal. Something very old hears it, and starts to move.

Side chapters · Mercury & Mars

Curse of Osiris introduces the exiled Warlock and the Vex's reality-simulating Infinite Forest. Warmind reawakens the Golden-Age war-AI with Ana Bray beneath Mars. Both seed threads that pay off years later.

Forsaken · 2018 The turning point

The Death of Cayde-6

A prison break in the Reef ends with murdered by , the unstable exiled prince of the Awoken. Hunting his killer across the lawless Tangled Shore, you're pulled into the Dreaming City, Queen 's hidden realm and the real hand behind it: , working through Riven, the last wish-granting Ahamkara.

You kill Uldren, but the City falls into an endless three-week time loop you can never quite break. This is also where you meet and his high-stakes game, Gambit and where Uldren's discarded Ghost finds his corpse, ready to raise him anew.

The death that changed the tone

Forsaken is the hinge of the whole series: the loss of Cayde turns a hopeful space-fantasy into a story about grief, revenge and, eventually, forgiveness.

Shadowkeep · 2019 The Darkness arrives

The Pyramid on the Moon

calls you back to the Moon, where a black Pyramid ship (a vessel of the Darkness itself) lies buried. It conjures Nightmares of the dead you've lost and, at its core, offers something stranger than a fight: a conversation.

The ancient enemy that hunted the Traveler hasn't just sent armies. One of its own vessels now sits in our system, and it has been waiting here a very long time.

Beyond Light · 2020 Stasis · first Dark power

Wielding the Enemy's Power

On Jupiter's frozen moon Europa, the exiled Fallen warlord bends the Darkness into a weapon: Stasis, the power of cosmic ice. To stop her you learn to wield it too (the first time Guardians turn the Darkness to their own ends) guided by the time-lost .

The lesson of Europa is uncomfortable: the Darkness isn't simply evil. It's a tool and now it's yours.

Universe change · four worlds vanish

As Beyond Light arrives, Mercury, Mars, Titan and Io disappear, the Witness using the Darkness to pull whole worlds into an unknown realm. The Leviathan drifts off around the same time. The system gets smaller, and the threat gets a lot more real.

The Witch Queen · 2022 Truth of the Light

Savathûn Steals the Light

returns wielding the Light, Hive raised by their own stolen Ghosts, the Lucent Brood and dares you into her storybook Throne World to learn how. The answer rewrites the whole war: she didn't steal the Light at all. The Traveler chose her.

You sever her bargain by killing her body, but her Ghost vanishes, and a warning lingers about the far greater power behind every Pyramid, now almost here.

Why it matters

If the Traveler can choose an enemy as monstrous as Savathûn, then Light and Darkness were never simply "good" and "evil." That single revelation is the philosophical hinge the whole saga turns on.

The Final Shape · 2024 Prismatic · Light + Dark

Into the Pale Heart

The chase leads inside the Traveler, into the Pale Heart, a dreamscape woven from memory, where a resurrected walks again at your side. Fusing Light and Dark into the Prismatic power, you rally a full coalition (humanity, the Eliksni, and Cabal) against the Witness's design.

In the raid Salvation's Edge and the climactic mission Excision, the assembled Guardians unravel the 's collective mind for good. After ten years, the Light and Darkness Saga ends but killing a being that old leaves a hole in the order of the cosmos.

Who the Witness was

The Witness's origin was actually revealed a year earlier, in Season of the Deep, through a vision shown by the leviathan Ahsa. Long ago the Traveler uplifted a mortal species and then moved on, leaving them behind. Desperate for a single fixed meaning, they found the Veil, used the Darkness to merge into one mind, and set out to freeze all of creation into one perfect "final shape." The villain of the whole saga was, at root, a people who could not bear an unfinished universe.

Saga Two · Year 8 · "The Year of Prophecy"

The Fate Saga

A new power vacuum, a new power source, dark matter and a prophecy of extinction no one is allowed to hear.

The Edge of Fate · July 2025 Dark matter

Bind the Nine

A strange summons from draws you to Kepler, a rim-world saturated with dark matter, a third cosmic force you learn to blend with Light and Dark. There, one of the Nine, Three (III), dies, and through the time-displaced delivers a prophecy: bind the Nine to the physical plane, or Sol faces extinction.

You learn the Nine have manipulated events for ages, even engineering 's original death and rebirth as a Guardian. And, fearing mass panic, and the Vanguard bury the prophecy.

The big twist

You (the Guardian) are hinted to be a weapon of the Nine, shaped for a purpose not yet revealed. The Vanguard's cover-up sets up the central conflict of everything that follows.

Furious at the cover-up, breaks with the Vanguard and sets up an outlaw base in Mars' Tharsis Cantina, gathering a crew: , the Eliksni , and a by-the-book Praxic Order Guardian, Aunor. Their enemy is the Barant Imperium, a Cabal legion under Premier Lume and . Bael is not a Guardian. Renegades is an official, Lucasfilm-collaborated Star Wars-styled chapter, the Praxic Order as Jedi, the Praxic Blade as lightsaber. It's Destiny telling a Star Wars story with its own lore, not a crossover.

Where Things Stand

The short version, if you're booting up today.

The Witness is dead and the ten-year war is won but its absence cracked the cosmic order. The Nine are unravelling, one of them is dead, and a buried prophecy says they must be bound to mortal flesh or the whole system dies.

The Vanguard is divided by its own cover-up. has set out to end the Hive pantheon for good, hunting her surviving "sisters," and , who are both still scheming and rebuilding. You, meanwhile, may be a weapon the Nine built long ago. And (who spent a lifetime keeping one foot out the door) finally stopped running, choosing to stay and fight beside the family he found.

The system itself has changed: Titan is back, the Eliksni live among us, the Cabal are half-friend and half-foe, and humanity's circle of allies is wider than ever. And after years adrift, the Traveler has returned to hover over the Last City, steadying an Earth that had begun to die when Three of the Nine fell.

The Final Update · June 9, 2026

Monument of Triumph

Less a new chapter than a memorial, a living museum in the Tower honouring everything Guardians have done: gods felled, blades shattered, Nightmares banished, a Witch Queen out-played at her own game.

Active development winds down here, and the live-service machinery stops. But the servers stay on, the Sol system stays open, and the whole ten-year legend remains yours to walk through, raid by raid, whenever you return.

The true last word · two hidden cutscenes

Bungie buried the real finale behind a checklist of Triumphs, so it's easy to miss. There are two secret ending cinematics, one a heartfelt goodbye, one a tease of what would have come next. They're worth unlocking yourself, but if you'd rather just know, tap to reveal.

Reveal Ending 1, the farewell spoiler

Reveal Ending 2, the cliffhanger spoiler

An "Unknown Voice", widely believed to be the Winnower, the primal Darkness itself, speaks through Lodi. It tells you to rest and enjoy beating the Witness, but to "stay hungry," hints the Nine only need Guardians because it exists, and promises an inevitable, far greater conflict, closing with: roughly, don't worry, when it's over, I won't leave you behind. With no Destiny 3 in development, it's a thread that may never be pulled.

Optional · For When You Log In

The Returning Guardian's Checklist

Coming back cold is overwhelming. Here's the short path through it.

  • Grab your welcome-back loot. Logging in drops 2 Bright Engrams, 50 Legendary Marks and an emblem; long-time accounts also get the "Old Lights" emblem and an emote.
  • Don't panic about your Power. Everyone now starts at Power 10. You climb to a base of 200 through normal play, and from there seasonal activities push you toward the current seasonal cap (450, rising to 550 late in the season). That seasonal power resets each season, so coming back mid-season is no problem.
  • If you're rusty on the basics, go to the Cosmodrome and run the New Light quest from Shaw Han, a quick refresher on movement, abilities and the Director.
  • Start the send-off. In the Tower courtyard, interact with the Monument in the centre to begin its onramp quest.
  • Learn the one loop that matters now: complete Triumphs from anywhere in the game's history → earn Legendary Marks → spend them at the four Tenet vendors in the courtyard (Bravery, Devotion, Sacrifice, Death). Marks never expire, so there's no rush.
  • Level up through the Portal. Any Ops count toward your climb, Solo, Fireteam, Pinnacle or Crucible, so play what you enjoy. There is no weekly pinnacle cap any more; you can grind as much or as little as you like.
  • Never solo-queue a raid. Use the in-game Fireteam Finder (from the roster screen) to group up, every campaign, raid and dungeon is now free to all.
  • New permanent things to try: Sparrow Racing is back, and Pantheon offers a rotating boss gauntlet for raiders.
  • Before you finish: chase the two hidden ending cutscenes above, they're the actual conclusion of the story.
